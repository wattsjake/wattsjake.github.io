NPN Transistor Amplifier

The common emitter amplifier has many uses as a general purpose amplifier. This emitter typically offers high current gain, medium input resistance and a high output resistance. The output of a common emitter amplifier is 180 degrees out of phase to the input signal.

There are three regions of a bipolar-junction-transistor (BJT).

  • Active (Forward-active): In this region the BJT can act as a fairly linear amplifier.
  • Saturation: The base current has increased well beyond the point that the emitter-base junction is forward biased. The transistor is ON.
  • Cut-off: This is the case where the transistor is essentially inactive. The transistor is OFF.
